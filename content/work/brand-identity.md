The brief was simple on paper: create a mark that felt handmade without looking homemade. The studio worked across ceramics, textiles, and print — disparate disciplines that needed a single thread to hold them together.

We started with process. Watching how objects were made in the studio — the deliberate pressure of a thumb into clay, the way linen holds a crease — gave us a visual language grounded in material honesty. The identity emerged from that, not from the brief.

The final system uses a single weight of a geometric sans paired with a custom stamp motif. Printed collateral is letterpress on uncoated stock. The restraint is intentional: the work itself should be the loudest thing in the room.

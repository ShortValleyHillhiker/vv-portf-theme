The publisher had been running on a template for six years. The content had outgrown it — a catalog of over four hundred titles, a quarterly journal, and a reading list that editors updated irregularly. The site was doing too many things and none of them well.

The redesign started by cutting. The reading list became a newsletter. The journal got its own subdomain. What remained was a clean catalog and a handful of editorial pages — the publisher's voice, undiluted.

Typographically, we leaned on the books themselves. Title pages from the back catalog informed the hierarchy. The grid is asymmetric and intentionally print-influenced, which felt right for a publisher that still cares about paper.
